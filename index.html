Kentaro Miura

Greatest Japanse Mangaka

"He died doing what he wanted, no matter what, right?... I bet he was happy." - Guts

Iconic photo of Kentaro Miura in his studio, where he works

He was born in Chiba City on July 11, 1966. He was left-handed. In 1976, at the age of 10, Miura made his first manga, titled Miuranger, published by his classmates at school. The manga reached 40 volumes. In 1977, Miura created his second manga called Ken e no michi, using Indian ink for the first time. When he was in high school in 1979, his drawing technique improved greatly as he began to use professional techniques. In 1988, Miura returned with a 48-page manga titled Berserk The Prototype, which was an introduction to the later well-known Berserk manga. This prototype led him to win an award from Comi Manga School. In 1989, Kentarou began a project titled Oh-Roh based on the script by Buronson, writer of Hokuto no Ken. It was published monthly in Japanese Animal House magazine in numbers 5 and 7 of that year.

In 1990, he made the sequel to Ourou titled Oh-Roh-Den which was published in Young Animal magazine. In that same year, issue 10 of Animal House published the first issue of the solo project Berserk and it was accepted by the public with moderate success. Miura again collaborated with Buronson to make the manga titled Japan where he drew but the story is Buronson's. It was published in Young Animal House from numbers 1 to 8 in 1992, and was later collected into a single volume. Miura's fame grew after Berserk was published, specifically the "Golden Age" arc. This brought him enormous success that has turned this work, for many, into his masterpiece and made him one of the most prominent contemporary manga artists. In the following years, Miura dedicated himself exclusively to working on Berserk.

In 1997, Miura supervised the production of the 25 episodes of the Berserk anime that aired that year on the NTV network. With the success of the anime, several art books and other new materials based on the Berserk universe were published. In 1999, Miura was the executive supervisor of the Dreamcast video game Sword of the Berserk: Guts' Rage, also creating the designs for the new characters and the story script. The year 2004 also contributed in a minor way to another video game, but now for the PS2 console called Berserk Millennium Falcon Arc: Chapter of the Holy Demon War, developing a new plot and creating characters.

Since then, the Berserk manga has spanned 40 volumes. The series has also spawned a whole host of merchandising, both official and fan-made, ranging from statues, action figures, keychains, video games, and a collectible card game. Kentaro Miura received the national Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize in the "Excellence" category for Berserk.
